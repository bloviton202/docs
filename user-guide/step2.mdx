## Edit Cut-out

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Purpose">
    <Card title="Restore or Refine Parts From Your Initial Image" icon="edit">
      **Edit Cut-out** allows you to **erase or restore parts** of your original image that may have been accidentally removed by the background remover.

      <Info>
        Use this tool to:

        - **Restore** missing elements (e.g., a lamp, rug, or part of a sofa)
        - **Remove** unwanted elements that were included in the cut-out
        - **Refine** edges for a cleaner, more precise outline
      </Info>
      <Warning>
        Perfect for fixing errors from automatic background removal.
      </Warning>
    </Card>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Best Practices">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Zoom In">
        Work at **high magnification** for precision editing.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restore Missing Parts">
        Use the **restore brush** to bring back parts of the product or scene that were mistakenly erased (e.g., a rug, lamp, or decorative element).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Remove Unwanted Elements">
        Use the **erase brush** to clean up any background remnants or extra objects.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Smooth Edges">
        Ensure **natural, flowing outlines** for a professional result.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Aspect Ratio

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Format Options">
    Choose the format of your final image based on your intended use:

    | Ratio | Best For           | Usage                          |
    | ----- | ------------------ | ------------------------------ |
    | 1:1   | Social media posts | Instagram, Facebook squares    |
    | 4:5   | Portrait posts     | Instagram stories, Pinterest   |
    | 16:9  | Landscape content  | Website headers, presentations |
    | 9:16  | Vertical videos    | TikTok, Instagram reels        |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Flexible Workflow">
    <Tip>
      Pro Tip: You can change the aspect ratio anytime after generation without regenerating the whole scene by using the Aspect Ratio Feature\!
    </Tip>
    Workflow Benefits:

    - Generate once, adapt to multiple formats
    - Save time and processing credits
    - Maintain consistent quality across ratios
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Framing Best Practices

<Warning>
  Proper framing is crucial for optimal AI generation results.
</Warning>

<Card title="The Two-Thirds Rule" icon="expand">
  Key Principle: Place your product large enough within the frame, ideally covering about two-thirds of the initial image size.

  Why This Matters:

  - Better product definition in final output
  - Prevents dimension or proportion issues
  - Ensures the AI focuses on your product
  - Maintains professional quality standards
</Card>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Framing Guidelines" icon="frame">
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Optimal Framing" icon="check" color="#green">
        - Product fills 60-70% of frame
        - Clear product boundaries
        - Adequate padding around edges
        - Maintains aspect ratio integrity
      </Card>
      <Card title="Avoid These Issues" icon="times" color="#red">
        - Product too small in frame
        - Cropped or cut-off elements
        - Excessive empty space
        - Off-center positioning
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quality Checklist" icon="list-check">
    <Check>
      Product covers approximately two-thirds of frame
    </Check>
    <Check>
      All important product features are visible
    </Check>
    <Check>
      Adequate margin space around product edges
    </Check>
    <Check>
      Consistent framing across multiple products
    </Check>
    <Check>
      Product is centered and well-balanced
    </Check>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
